Rivermark Hosts Hydro Innovation Summit

Faculty, students, and industry partners unveiled prototypes for resilient riverfront infrastructure and community-centered design.

The HydroUrban Innovation Lab welcomed 600 delegates to the inaugural Hydro Innovation Summit, an immersive convening that bridged civil engineering with ecological stewardship. Attendees toured the newly retrofitted RiverWorks Hall, featuring interactive simulations of flood adaptive neighborhoods.

Panels covered policy frameworks for equitable water access, while design studios showcased modular levee technologies developed in collaboration with Rivermark's School of Computing & Data Sciences. The summit concluded with the launch of the River Guardian Fellowship, an initiative providing student researchers with fieldwork stipends.

"Our river valley is a living laboratory," said Professor Liora Naveen, summit chair. "We are uniquely positioned to prototype solutions that uplift communities along waterways worldwide."
